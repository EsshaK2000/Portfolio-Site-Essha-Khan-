Pet Vet Case Study

Project Overview

What is Pet Vet?

Pet Vet is a veterinarian telemedicine application that connects pet owners with experienced veterinarians and provides easy access to veterinarian care-related information.

Designed to enhance convenience and accessibility, the application provides concise profiles of independent veterinarians and operational animal clinics and hospitals, virtual appointments, service estimate inquiries, side-by-side prescription and treatment cost rates, and a file management tool.

Pet Vet empowers pet owners to feel in financial control of their vet expenses while providing timely care for their cherished companions.

Problem and Design Challenge

How might we design a solution that enhances accessibility and transparency of veterinarian care, while providing pet owners with reliable information and fostering trust?

Currently, pet owners have to manually research extensively through the internet to find information on veterinary practices including services, treatments, and reviews, leading to information overload and search fatigue.

The Solutions

Simplifying the Search Process.

  • Simplify and streamline the process of finding veterinary care by offering a list of independent veterinarians and stand-alone animal clinics/hospitals in the area.
  • Access to virtual veterinary care from a diverse network of qualified veterinarians.
  • Condensed profiles containing essential information.
  • Eliminates the need for extensive online searching and filtering.

Veterinarian telemedicine without commitment.

  • Book virtual consultations with veterinarians without commitment to a subscription or a pre-existing relationship with a hospital, clinic, or veterinarian.
  • Book virtual consultations with vets.
  • Request quotes for services/treatments from local animal clinics.
  • Flexible appointment options and pricing.

Cost transparency of veterinary services.

  • Cost transparency by offering price lists, hourly rates, and price comparison features based on location.
  • Offering price lists, hourly rates, and price comparison features based on location.
  • Find and compare clinics through the cost of specific treatments.
  • Find and compare pharmacies through the cost of prescribed medication.

White Paper Research

3 in 5 Canadian pet owners believe their vet charged too much.

I used white paper research to better understand the problem space by discovering factual evidence on the relationship between the veterinarian industry and its consumers (pet owners). In return, this helped me define the scope of my problem statement and solutions.

Competitive Analysis + The Gap

Upon conducting a SWOT Analysis on 4 veterinarian telemedicine apps, I recognized a weakness among my competitors.

I learned that:

  • Lacked cost transparency
  • Restricted access due to subscription-based models or required to commit to a specific hospital or clinic already using their software.
  • Lacked crucial information about independent veterinarians or standalone clinics/hospitals.

Through analyzing the competition, I strategized how to position my solutions to address the critical gaps overlooked by competitors:

  • Implementation of a transparent pricing model
  • Remphasize open access giving users the ability to book and complete virtual consultations easily within the app.
  • Provide comprehensive veterinary information regarding independent/affiliated veterinarians and clinics, including detailed profiles, reviews, and integrated contact information.

User Surveys

I then reached out to 20 pet owners and had them complete my user survey.

Assessing pet owners' attitudes and behaviors towards the veterinarian care industry was particularly insightful as it shed light on their encounters, perceptions, expectations, and pain points when seeking veterinarian care for their pets.

Through my survey research goals, I aimed to understand and:

  • Explore the overall sentiment of pet owners regarding veterinary care.

Results

  • Average of $500 spent per year in vet costs amongst 20 survey respondents.
  • Cost. Location. Experience. Top 3 most important factors when choosing a vet amongst survey respondents.
  • High Costs. Waitlists. Pet Anxiety. Top 3 most common pain points amongst survey respondents.

User Interviews

Following the user survey results, I conducted in-depth user interviews with 3 pet owners.

While the survey had identified users' pain points, the interviews helped uncover the underlying context and reasoning behind them. The interviews offered a contextual understanding and insight into how users interact with veterinarian services in their day-to-day lives, shedding light on the real-life experiences and factors that influence their current perceptions and feelings.

My user interview research goals included:

  • Investigate users' perceptions and emotional associations with veterinary care.
  • Explore how telemedicine applications fulfill users' functional needs, replacing physical methods for routine tasks.
  • Assess participants' comfort level and familiarity with adopting digital veterinary telemedicine services for long-term use.

The Main Conclusion from User Surveys & User Interviews

I found that despite pet owners' high regard for transparency in their pet's healthcare decision-making process, the veterinary industry is currently falling short in providing for this need.

These themes provide valuable insights into user perspectives and expectations regarding veterinary care, pointing to areas for improvement in communication, transparency, convenience, and organization within the veterinary industry and pet ownerâ€™s lifestyles.

User Flows & Information Architecture

Exploring user Flows and organizing structure.

The problem I aimed to address with the user flow design method was to ensure a seamless and effective user experience for pet owners seeking veterinary telemedicine services and information through my app.

I learned where users might encounter difficulties, such as searching for a suitable veterinarian or clinic for their pet or understanding how to initiate a teleconsultation. This made me more aware of more complex flows and solve how to effectively break them down into digestible processes.

Low fidelity to High Fidelity Wireframes

The blueprints of my design.

The low-fidelity and mid-fidelity wireframes served as a tangible foundation, allowing for rapid exploration of layout, content placement, and user flows. This iterative approach encouraged me to be creative when problem-solving complex user flows and identify potential design flaws at an early stage.

The grey scale high fidelity wireframes served as the wireframes of the prototype to be used in my usability study. It is a semi-polished yet neutral representation of the design, allowing study participants to focus solely on layout, structure, and content hierarchy without the distraction of aesthetics. This approach ensures that design decisions are based on the fundamental visual elements and user experience, promoting better usability and functionality assessment.

Final Prototype

Usability Study + Improvments

Major Improvements in my design.

Usability Issue 1

  • Participants began losing interest in the onboarding.
  • Improvement: I divided the onboarding process into smaller, more manageable screens to create a step-by-step account setup experience. This approach aims to prevent users from feeling overwhelmed by having to enter all their information at once.

Usability Issue 2

  • 4/6 participants were unable to recollect their appointment booking details as they approached the end of the booking process.
  • Improvement:I introduced a confirmation of booking details screen midway through the booking process to ensure that users are informed and content with their selections made in the prior booking screens.

Usability Issue 3

  • 2/6 participants expressed that they would have liked to see an explanation of their selected treatment. They expressed a preference for this information to be readily available within the app, rather than having to resort to external sources like Google to understand its meaning.
  • Improvement: I added an additional screen that explains the treatmentâ€™s purpose and how itâ€™s performed.
